Summary of Renewable Energy Types

Solar Energy

Solar energy is harnessed by capturing sunlight and heat from the sun using photovoltaic panels or solar collectors. It is a clean and renewable energy source that can be used to generate electricity or heat water.

Wind Energy

Wind energy is generated by using wind turbines to capture the kinetic energy of wind. It is a renewable energy source that does not produce emissions during operation and is increasingly cost-competitive.

Hydropower

Hydropower, or hydroelectric power, is generated by harnessing the potential energy of water stored in dams or the kinetic energy of flowing water. It is a renewable energy source and one of the most widely used worldwide.

Biomass Energy

Biomass energy is produced from organic materials such as agricultural residues, forest waste, and organic waste. This energy can be used to generate electricity, heat, or biofuels, contributing to waste reduction and lower greenhouse gas emissions.

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy is obtained by harnessing heat from within the Earth. It is mainly used for electricity generation and heating. It is a renewable energy source that offers consistent and reliable production.
